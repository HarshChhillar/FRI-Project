Forest Research Institute

The Forest Research Institute is a Natural Resource Service training institut e of the Indian Council of Forestry Research and Education and is an institution in the field of forestry research in India for Indian Forest Service cadres and all State Forest Service cadres.

Cource For Forest Research

Discover the secrets of the woods at the Forest Research Institute with courses tailored to unveil the intricate ecology and management of forest ecosystems. Delve into forestry science, conservation, and sustainable practices in these immersive educational offerings.

Under Graduate Cource

The Forest Research Institute (FRI) offers a diverse range of bachelor's courses catering to various aspects of forestry and related fields. These programs are designed to provide comprehensive knowledge and practical skills for students aspiring to pursue careers in forestry and environmental sciences. Some of the bachelor's courses offered at FRI include Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Forestry, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Wildlife Science, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Environmental Science, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Wood Science & Technology, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Agriculture, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Horticulture, Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Silviculture, and Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Agroforestry. These programs cover a wide spectrum of subjects including forest management, wildlife conservation, environmental sustainability, wood technology, agriculture, and horticulture, providing students with a solid foundation to contribute to the conservation and sustainable management of forest resources.

Post Graduate Cource

The Forest Research Institute (FRI) offers an array of postgraduate courses tailored to meet the advanced academic and professional needs of individuals seeking specialization in forestry and allied disciplines. Among the postgraduate courses available at FRI are the Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Forestry, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Wildlife Science, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Environmental Science, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Wood Science & Technology, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Agriculture, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Horticulture, Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Silviculture, and Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Agroforestry. These programs delve deeper into the theoretical and practical aspects of forest management, wildlife conservation, environmental sustainability, wood technology, agriculture, and horticulture, equipping students with specialized knowledge and skills necessary for leadership roles in research, conservation, and sustainable management of forest ecosystems. Additionally, FRI offers doctoral programs (Ph.D.) for those interested in pursuing advanced research and contributing to the advancement of knowledge in forestry and related fields

PHD Cource

At the Forest Research Institute (FRI), doctoral programs (Ph.D.) are offered to scholars seeking to delve deeply into research and contribute significantly to the advancement of knowledge in forestry and related disciplines. FRI provides an array of Ph.D. courses catering to diverse interests and specializations within the field. These Ph.D. programs include Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Forestry, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Wildlife Science, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Environmental Science, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Wood Science & Technology, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Agriculture, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Horticulture, Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Silviculture, and Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Agroforestry. These programs are designed to facilitate in-depth research, critical analysis, and scholarly exploration of topics ranging from forest ecology and biodiversity conservation to sustainable forestry practices, timber technology, agroforestry systems, and environmental policy. Through these Ph.D. programs, FRI aims to foster a community of researchers and experts equipped to address the complex challenges facing forest ecosystems and contribute to their sustainable management and conservation on a global scale.

FRI Campus

The Forest Research Institute (FRI) in Dehradun, India, boasts a sprawling campus nestled in the picturesque foothills of the Himalayas. Renowned for its architectural grandeur and vast botanical gardens, the FRI campus serves as a hub for forestry research, education, and conservation efforts.

Admission Procedure at FRI Dehradun:

  • Check eligibility criteria for the desired program on the official FRI Dehradun website
  • Fill out the online application form with personal, academic, and contact details.
  • Upload required documents such as mark sheets, certificates, and identification proof.
  • Pay the application fee online through the provided payment gateway.
  • Submit the application form and keep the acknowledgment for future reference
  • Await communication from FRI regarding entrance exams (if applicable) and further admission procedures.
  • Attend the counseling session or interview as scheduled by FRI.
  • Upon selection, complete the admission formalities, including fee payment and document verification.
  • Commence studies at FRI Dehradun and embark on a journey of academic excellence in forestry research and education
